# D2C SuperStore Sales Analytics

## Project Overview
This project analyzes a Direct-to-Consumer (D2C) retail superstore dataset to uncover key business insights across sales, profitability, customer behavior, and product performance.

The goal is to transform raw transactional data into actionable insights that help leadership teams make data-driven decisions.

---

## Business Problem
Retail businesses generate massive amounts of transactional data but often struggle to answer:

- Why is revenue growing but profit not scaling proportionally?
- Which products drive profit vs cause losses?
- Which customer segments should be targeted?
- How do payment and shipping choices impact operations?

---

## Key Business Questions

- Which categories and sub-categories drive the highest revenue and profit?
- Why did **December 2020 have higher sales but lower profit vs 2019?**
- Which products are causing **negative margins**?
- Which customer segment contributes the most revenue?
- How does payment mode impact cash flow efficiency?

## Key Insights

- 📈 Sales grew **77% YoY**, but profit grew only **14%**
- ⚠️ December 2020 showed **higher sales but lower profit**
- 💸 Tables sub-category generated **negative profit**
- 👥 Consumer segment contributed **48% of total revenue**
- 💳 **42.6% orders via COD**, impacting cash flow
- 🚚 **58% orders via Standard Shipping**

---

## Business Recommendations

- Optimize **discount strategy during peak months** to avoid margin erosion from over-discounting high-demand products
- Reduce **returns in Technology category** to minimize reverse logistics cost & profit leakage
- Re-evaluate **loss-making products like Tables** to fix pricing or eliminate negative-margin items
- Push **digital payments to reduce COD dependency** to improve cash flow & reduce COD operational risk
- Focus marketing on **high-performing products & segments** fo accelerated growth
